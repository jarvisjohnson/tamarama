Property Overview

Air, Sky, Water

Twenty One Thompson makes the most of the natural environment with continuously unfolding views of the Pacific Ocean. The house is also only metres from the vibrant amenity and parkland of Tamarama Beach, and within easy reach of local cafés, groceries and services.

Architect Wallace E. Cunningham has dedicated most of his career to designing residences. His fascination with coastal living spaces has resulted in refined expressions of his philosophy of movement and light, and this is the reason why he was chosen to design Twenty One Thompson, on a site where land meets the ocean and sky. What could be more Sydney?

“Metres from Tamarama Beach, and within easy reach of local cafés, groceries and services.”

The council approved plans include five bedrooms, multiple living spaces, a home theatre, gymnasium and wine room - all connected via a lift. With a perfect north facing aspect Cunningham’s design creates galleries of sky and water at each level, with spaces for indoor and outdoor entertainment, luxe and low-key leisure activities, work, family time and rest.

Architect

Wallace E. Cunningham

“Known for some of the most iconic houses along the Californian coast, his architecture also embodies the best of Sydney life.”

Wallace E. Cunningham lives on the other side of the Pacific in San Diego, and provides architectural, landscape and interior design services. Since its founding in 1979, his firm has gained international recognition as a leader in architectural design and has featured regularly in Architectural Digest’s Top 100 Designers list.

Cunningham is known for some of the most iconic houses along the Californian coast, but his architecture also embodies the best of Sydney life, celebrating the vitality offered by a connection to city and sea.

Wallace’s architecture works with the natural landscape rather than against it. When U2’s guitarist The Edge was unable to get residential plans approved for his property on the coast of California, he threw out the plans and hired Wallace to design architectural forms that blended naturally into the landscape.

Put simply, Wallace E. Cunningham’s site- specific architecture celebrates the environment, is designed with living in mind and creates distinctive, timeless forms.

Neighbourhood

Explore Tamarama

Seven kilometres east of the CBD, Tamarama is nestled between Bondi and Bronte. A smaller ocean cove, Tamarama is known as the glamourous younger sister of the neighbouring beaches. A micro-neighbourhood with everything, Tamarama’s amenities cover the luxuries as well as the essentials.

Strolling distance from both Bondi and Bronte beaches, shops and cafés, Tamarama is well- connected and central, while offering a distinctive neighbourhood feel, complete with extensive parkland and outdoor amenity.
