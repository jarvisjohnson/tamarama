Property Overview

Air, Sky, Water

Metres from the beach. Minutes from anything you need. Ocean for as far as the eye can see.

Twenty One Thompson makes the most of the natural environment with continuously unfolding views of the Pacific Ocean. The house is also only metres from the sand, surf and parkland of Tamarama Beach, and within easy reach of local cafés, groceries and services.

Architect Wallace E. Cunningham has dedicated most of his career to designing world class residences. His fascination with coastal living spaces has resulted in refined expressions of his philosophy of movement and light, and this is the reason why he was chosen to design Twenty One Thompson, on a site where land meets the ocean and sky. What could be more Sydney?

Metres from the beach. Minutes from anything you need. Ocean for as far as the eye can see.

The council approved plans include five bedrooms, multiple living spaces, a home theatre and gymnasium – all connected via a lift. With a perfect north facing aspect, Cunningham’s design creates galleries of sky and water at each level, with spaces for indoor and outdoor entertainment, luxe and low-key leisure activities, work, family time and rest.

The Architect

Wallace E. Cunningham

Regularly featured in Architectural Digest's Top 100 Designers list, Cunningham
is an internationally renowned architect whose work
also embodies the best of Sydney life.

Wallace E. Cunningham lives on the other side of the Pacific in San Diego, and provides architectural, landscape and interior design services to clients around the world.  Since its founding in 1979, his firm has gained international recognition as a leader in architectural design and has featured regularly in Architectural Digest’s Top 100 Designers list.

Cunningham is known for some of the most remarkable houses along the Californian coast, but his architecture is also perfectly suited to Sydney, a city whose lifestyle is built around the sea.

Wallace’s architecture works with the natural landscape rather than against it. When U2’s guitarist The Edge was unable to get residential plans approved for his property on the coast of California, he threw out the plans and hired Wallace to design architectural forms that blended naturally into the landscape.

Put simply, Wallace E. Cunningham’s site-specific architecture celebrates the environment, is designed with living in mind and creates distinctive, timeless forms.

Neighbourhood

Explore Tamarama

Eight kilometres east of the CBD, Tamarama is nestled between Bondi and Bronte. A smaller ocean cove, Tamarama is a prestigious area that has maintained a distinctly local feel. A micro-neighbourhood with everything, Tamarama’s amenities cover the luxuries as well as the essentials.

Strolling distance from both Bondi and Bronte beaches, shops and cafés, Tamarama is well-connected and central, while offering a distinctive neighbourhood feel, complete with extensive parkland, outdoor gyms and the most spectacular coastal walk in Australia.
